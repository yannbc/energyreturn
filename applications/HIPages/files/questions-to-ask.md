# Questions To Ask

Pre-composed. Drop in when there's air.

---

## Opening (after pleasantries)
> "Before we get into me, can I ask — where is the current pressure point on the team you are hiring into? That helps me give you the most useful version of my background."

## About the role
> "I noticed the role I applied to is one of several enterprise-facing product roles you are hiring. How are you thinking about the split between platform product work and SuperGTM-specific product work?"

> "Paul Staelin coming in as CCO is a recent signal. How is that changing the product motion — particularly around what gets built versus what gets configured for customers?"

## About the next step
> "If this conversation moves forward, what does the next step look like? Founder chat, take-home, both?"

> "Anything specific the founders care about hearing in the next conversation? I would rather come prepared than discover it on the call."

## Genuine curiosity (signals you've done the reading)
> "The Programmatic GTM mode where Rellies are now building out of Claude Code or Cursor — is that an internal shift in how agents get built, or is that being productised for customers?"

## If asked "anything else?" at the end
Use the SuperGTM/platform split question (above) if you haven't already.

---

## Don't ask
- Anything about Glassdoor culture (let them raise it)
- Anything that sounds like "do you really need this role" (sounds skeptical)
- Anything about comp (deflect to the founder conversation)
- Anything about the Salesforce/Agentforce threat (save it for founders)
