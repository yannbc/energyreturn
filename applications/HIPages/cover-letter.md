# hipages — Senior Vice President of Product

**Applied:** April 2026

---

The reason this role lands for me is that hipages is at the point in its arc where product leadership stops being about setting strategy and starts being about whether the org can actually ship against one. I've lived that transition as CPTO at Pendula, running a 20-person product and engineering organisation through to acquisition by Smart Communications. The binding constraint was never "what should we build." It was "how do we ship it before the theory of the case expires." We moved the org from architecture-perfect releases to experiment-first — and that posture delivered the $5M ARR uplift and the acquisition outcome.

On the marketplace and platform side: at Billcap I ran a multi-tenant SaaS for five enterprise energy retailers with 250,000+ end users, exited at 8x investor return. At DC Power Co I built customer engagement and cross-sell product for a two-sided energy retailer where every product decision had to serve both supply and homeowners. Both businesses taught me how bundles actually stay sticky — and where they quietly don't.

On the AI-as-efficiency story: the narrative ASX-listed tech boards keep asking for is the same one — R&D as a percentage of revenue down, without cutting R&D, which means revenue per engineer has to go up. The most valuable Pendula AI features were customer-facing product unlocks and internal productivity unlocks at the same time. The product leader who lands that dual narrative — for the people shipping it and the people funding it — is more valuable than the role title suggests.
