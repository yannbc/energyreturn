## Division of Labour: Tasklet vs Cowork

**Tasklet** (the agent running at tasklet.ai) handles the daily scan:
- Searches LinkedIn, Indeed and job aggregators at 7am AEST daily
- Scores and deduplicates roles against a pipeline database
- Emails a digest to yann@energyreturn.co with new matches
- Maintains the pipeline database and job-search-log.md in the repo

**Cowork** (you) handles application production:
- Yann forwards roles from the Tasklet digest (or finds them independently)
- You produce the full application pack: brief, cover letter, tailored CV
- Follow the five-step workflow below for each role

**Do NOT duplicate the scanning work.** Tasklet has browser-based LinkedIn access and a deduplication database. Your job starts when a role lands in front of you.

## Workflow for Each New Role

When a new role is provided for application, follow these steps:

### Step 1: Research the Role

- Read the full job description carefully. Identify the top 3--5 requirements.
- Research the company: recent news, strategy shifts, leadership changes, funding rounds, M&A activity.
- Identify **why the role exists now**. What changed? New leader? New strategy? Someone left? Growth pressure? This is the intelligence that makes the application stand out.
- Research the hiring manager if named. LinkedIn, press mentions, conference talks.

### Step 2: Assess Fit

- Score the role 1--10 against Yann's profile. Be honest about gaps.
- Identify the strongest 2--3 experience-to-requirement mappings.
- Flag any risks: salary, seniority mismatch, location, experience gaps.
- Identify the single most compelling angle -- the thing that makes this application different from every other senior AI/product leader applying.

### Step 3: Write the Cover Letter

- Max 4 paragraphs. No waffle.
- **Opening:** Something specific about the company/role that shows research, not generic interest. Reference the "why now" intelligence if possible.
- **Middle paragraphs:** Map Yann's experience to their specific requirements. Use concrete metrics and outcomes (8x return, $5M ARR, 25% churn reduction, NPS +51, 250K users). Don't list everything -- pick the 2--3 most relevant proof points.
- **Close:** Brief, confident, no begging. "I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss this further."
- **Sign off:** "Kind regards, Yann Burden"

### Step 4: Tailor the CV

- Start from the appropriate base CV below (data science variant or consulting variant -- pick whichever is closest, and note which you chose).
- Modify the **Highlights** section to emphasise what matters for this specific role.
- Modify the **Focus** keywords.
- Adjust bullet points within **Experience** sections to foreground the most relevant achievements.
- Do NOT invent experience or metrics that aren't in the source material.
- Do NOT use em dashes.
- Keep to 2 pages maximum when rendered.

### Step 5: Write Application Notes

- Fit score with brief rationale
- Flags or risks (salary, seniority, gaps)
- Suggested next steps (apply direct, reach out to someone first, wait for more info)
- Priority ranking relative to other active applications if applicable
